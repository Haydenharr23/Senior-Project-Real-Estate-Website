Price is the most powerful lever you control as a seller. Here's what happens at each end of the spectrum.

Too Low
  • Leaves money on the table
  • Buyers may assume something is wrong
  • Harder to negotiate back up
Just Right
  • Attracts serious, qualified buyers
  • Generates competitive offers
  • Sells within weeks, not months
  • Maximizes your net proceeds
Too High
  • Scares buyers away immediately
  • Home sits on the market longer
  • Price reductions signal weakness

Inspection & Pricing

Working with Michael

Strong offers are about more than price. Use the topics below to think through your net walkaway and negotiation levers — then lean on Michael when it is time to respond.

What affects your net proceeds?

“Net” is roughly sale price minus mortgage payoff, commissions, title fees, prorated taxes, and any agreed seller credits or repairs — each contract line matters.

  • Ask for a seller net sheet before you list so you are not surprised at closing.
  • Prepay penalties or second liens reduce what you take home — disclose them early.
  • Buyer-requested closing cost credits come out of your side; weigh them against a higher offer with fewer credits.
CMA vs. appraisal (for your buyer’s loan)

Your list price is informed by a comparative market analysis (CMA). The buyer’s lender still orders an independent appraisal. They usually align, but not always.

  • If an appraisal falls short, you may reduce price, meet in the middle, or risk the deal — your agent will map options.
  • Clean presentation and real comps up front reduce appraisal drama.
Common incentives buyers ask for

Buyers may request rate buy-downs, closing cost credits, home warranties, or personal property. Each has a dollar value — compare apples to apples when reviewing multiple offers.

  • A lower offer with zero credits can net more than a higher offer loaded with concessions.
  • Deadlines and financing type (conventional vs. FHA/VA) affect certainty, not just price.
Counter-offer vs. accept

You can accept, reject, or counter any offer. A counter restarts the clock on certain response periods — strategy matters in multi-offer situations.

  • Prioritize your non-negotiables (close date, rent-back, inspection cap).
  • Michael will help you craft counters that protect your net without chasing buyers away.
