Why Mortgage Pre-Approval Matters

Pre-approval is one of the smartest steps you can take before touring homes—it defines your budget and strengthens your offer.

Know your numbers

A lender reviews income, debts, and credit to estimate how much you can borrow and at what terms. That helps you shop in the right price range and avoid disappointment.

Show sellers you are ready

In a multiple-offer situation, a pre-approval letter signals that a lender has already vetted your finances. Some sellers will not even consider offers without it.

Faster path to closing

Much of the paperwork starts early, so you are not scrambling after your offer is accepted. Your loan officer can explain timelines and any documents to gather now.
